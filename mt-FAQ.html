Q: Why are some of the exercises repetitive?

A: The app is designed to develop the habit of flexible thinking through repeated training. We believe repetition is necessary for building new thinking skills. Changing our thinking habits takes time, so be patient and stick with it! Watch "Why MindTrails Involves Repetition" by graduate student Alexandra Silverman to learn more.

Q: Why do some scenarios have a negative ending?

A: The purpose of MindTrails is to encourage flexibility between positive, negative, and neutral thoughts. As you practice, you will learn that there are many different ways a situation can be interpreted, and the worst-case scenario is only one of them. Watch "Why MindTrails Doesn't Have All Stories End Positively" by graduate student Jeremy Eberle to learn more.

Q: What do I do if I'm not receiving app notifications?

A: There are a few suggestions! Make sure your notifications are enabled. To do this, go to the settings app on your phone and click on MindTrails. Then select to allow for notifications.

We have also found that notifications are most reliable when the MindTrails app is running in the background of your phone at all times. If you tend to swipe out of your apps, we would recommend that you avoid doing that for MindTrails.

Q: What is CBM-I?

A: CBM-I stands for cognitive bias modification of interpretations. This is a strategy commonly used to try and modify the cognitive biases that are often present in individuals that struggle with anxiety. These biases may manifest when individuals automatically interpret certain ambiguous stimuli in a negative way. With CBM-I, individuals undergo certain repetitive training in an attempt to alter their automatic negative thought processes and promote flexible thinking patterns.
